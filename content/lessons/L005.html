Lesson 005 – Number System Conversions

Level: Beginner | Stage: Digital Electronics | Duration: 10–15 minutes

Concept Overview

Number system conversion is the process of converting a number from one base (radix) to another. In digital electronics, conversions are required because humans prefer decimal representation, while hardware operates using binary.

Engineers also use octal and hexadecimal representations to simplify binary values during design, debugging, and verification.

Binary to Decimal Conversion

Each binary digit is multiplied by powers of 2 based on its position.

Example:
(1101)2 = 1×2³ + 1×2² + 0×2¹ + 1×2⁰ = 1310

Decimal to Binary Conversion

Decimal numbers are converted to binary by repeatedly dividing the number by 2 and recording the remainders.

Example:
(25)10 = (11001)2

Binary to Octal Conversion

Binary numbers are grouped into sets of three bits starting from the right.

Example:
(101101)2 = (55)8

Binary to Hexadecimal Conversion

Binary numbers are grouped into sets of four bits starting from the right.

Example:
(11110000)2 = (F0)16

Why Hexadecimal is Preferred

  • Shorter and more readable representation
  • Direct mapping to binary
  • Commonly used in memory addressing
  • Simplifies debugging and verification

Key Takeaways

  • Binary is the base of all digital systems
  • Grouping simplifies octal and hexadecimal conversions
  • Conversions are essential for RTL and debugging

Self-Test

1. Convert (101011)2 to decimal.

2. Convert (64)10 to binary.

3. Convert (11110000)2 to hexadecimal.

Self-Test Solutions

Answer 1: 4310

Answer 2: (1000000)2

Answer 3: (F0)16
