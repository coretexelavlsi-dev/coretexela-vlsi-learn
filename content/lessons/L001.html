L001: Introduction to Digital Electronics

Digital electronics forms the foundation of all modern computing systems. It operates using discrete signal levels instead of continuous values.

Concept Intuition

Unlike analog systems, which vary smoothly, digital systems operate using only two states: 0 and 1. This binary approach enables high reliability, noise immunity, and scalability.

Digital systems intentionally restrict signals to two voltage ranges to ensure predictable behavior and robust hardware operation.

Worked Example: Decision Making in Hardware

Consider a temperature-controlled fan. Instead of reacting to every small fluctuation, the system uses a threshold to decide ON or OFF.
