L001: Introduction to Digital Electronics

Digital electronics is the foundation of all modern computing systems. It operates using binary signals rather than continuous values.

Concept Intuition

Unlike analog systems that vary smoothly, digital systems work with only two states: 0 and 1. This makes digital circuits more reliable, noise-resistant, and scalable.

Formal Definition

Digital Electronics is the study of electronic systems that use discrete signal levels to represent information, typically using binary numbers.

Worked Example

A light switch is a real-world digital system:

  • OFF → 0
  • ON → 1

Applications

  • Microprocessors
  • Memory systems
  • Embedded controllers
  • Communication devices

Checkpoint

Q: Why are digital systems preferred over analog?

A: They are more robust, easier to design, and less affected by noise.
