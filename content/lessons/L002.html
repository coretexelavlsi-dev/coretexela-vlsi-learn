Lesson 002 – Applications of Chips

Level: Beginner | Stage: VLSI Foundations | Duration: 10–15 minutes

Concept Overview

Integrated circuits, commonly known as chips, are the core components of modern electronic systems. They perform computation, control, communication, and data processing.

Without chips, today’s digital devices would be bulky, slow, unreliable, and inefficient.

Major Application Areas

  • Consumer Electronics: Smartphones, laptops, smart TVs, wearables
  • Automotive: Engine control units, infotainment, ADAS systems
  • Data Centers & AI: CPUs, GPUs, AI accelerators
  • Healthcare: Medical imaging and monitoring devices
  • Industrial & IoT: Sensors, robotics, automation systems

Industry Perspective

Each application area has different design requirements such as performance, power consumption, area, and reliability.

VLSI engineers design chips that are optimized for specific use cases, making application knowledge extremely important.

Key Takeaways

  • Chips power all modern electronic systems
  • Different applications require different chip architectures
  • VLSI enables intelligence, automation, and connectivity
  • Application knowledge connects theory to real products

Self-Test

1. Name any two applications of chips.

2. Why do AI chips differ from mobile processor chips?

Self-Test Solutions

Answer 1: Smartphones, automobiles, data centers, medical devices (any two)

Answer 2: AI chips focus on high parallel computation and throughput.
